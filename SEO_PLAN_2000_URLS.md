## Publishing schedule

### Phase 1 — Foundation and pre-release authority (July 17–31)

Target: 35–60 indexed URLs.

- Finish release, platform, editions, story, mechanics, world structure and named-character pages.
- Add only entities shown in official articles, store listings and attributable previews.
- Build GSC/GA4 baselines and submit `/sitemap-index.xml` after deployment.
- Capture weekly query clusters: release date, platform, price, multiplayer, open world, requirements, characters and combat.

### Phase 2 — Evidence-ready architecture (August 1–31)

Target: 120–250 indexed URLs; prepare non-indexed data structures for the launch expansion.

- Build database schemas and hub templates for quests, locations, items, enemies, bosses, abilities and collectibles.
- Publish named entities only when the page can answer a distinct query.
- Prepare screenshot naming, source logging, last-reviewed dates and duplicate-intent checks.
- Test controlled batches of 20–40 pages and monitor discovered/crawled/indexed ratios for seven days.

### Phase 3 — Review/launch evidence sprint (September 1–23)

Target: 800–1,400 quality URLs if review or verified play access supplies enough evidence.

- Populate exact objectives, stats, map locations, rewards, prerequisites and failure states.
- Prioritize main quests, bosses, abilities, weapon forms, zone maps and high-friction puzzles.
- Release in batches of no more than 80–100 candidates per day, with automated and representative human QA.
- Pause any template whose pages are thin, duplicative or poorly indexed.

### Phase 4 — Launch completion (September 24 onward)

Target: reach 2,000 approved URLs after remaining facts and player pain points are verified.

- Fill side quests, collectibles, items, enemy drops, builds, endings and troubleshooting.
- Use GSC queries and community questions to prioritize pages players actually need.
- Update pre-release wording, dates and status stamps across the site.
- Merge or noindex pages that cannibalize a stronger answer.

## Page quality gate

A page is indexable only if all checks pass:

1. One distinct search intent and an immediate concrete answer.
2. Accurate title, meta description, H1 and self-canonical.
3. Verified facts; no invented names, stats, coordinates, requirements or rewards.
4. Sufficient unique utility: steps, data, comparison, exact location or troubleshooting—not generic prose.
5. Breadcrumbs plus links to its hub and genuinely adjacent pages.
6. Relevant structured data, where applicable, without unsupported FAQ or review markup.
7. No duplicate/cannibalizing route, broken internal link or empty variant.
8. Mobile-readable main content and successful build/type/SEO checks.
9. Source and last-reviewed date recorded internally or visibly where helpful.
10. Representative manual review before each batch enters the sitemap.

## Weekly operating dashboard

Track: total approved URLs, sitemap URLs, discovered URLs, crawled URLs, indexed URLs, 7-day and 28-day impressions/clicks/CTR/position, pages with zero impressions after 28 days, duplicate-canonical exclusions, crawl errors, and the percentage of pages passing the concrete-answer review.
