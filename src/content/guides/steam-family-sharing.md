## Quick answer

**Yes. Control Resonant's Steam listing includes Family Sharing.** Eligible members of a Steam Family can access a shareable copy under Steam's current rules, subject to account, region, simultaneous-use and publisher restrictions.

## What this does not mean

Family Sharing is not a free extra copy that can be permanently separated from the purchaser's library. It also does not create cross-buy entitlement on Xbox, Epic, PlayStation or Mac App Store.

## Can two people play the same copy simultaneously?

Steam Families applies library-copy availability rules. If the family owns one eligible copy, simultaneous access to that same title may be limited; additional owned copies increase availability. The storefront feature badge confirms eligibility, while Valve's current family rules govern use.

## Are saves shared?

Each Steam account normally maintains its own save and achievement context where supported. Control Resonant's exact Steam Cloud label is not present in the retrieved feature list, so cloud-save behavior should not be promised solely from Family Sharing support.

## Does Deluxe content share?

The base product is labeled for Family Sharing, but the inheritance of pre-order bonuses, soundtrack, artbook and other Deluxe entitlements can depend on Steam's DLC-sharing rules and the publisher's configuration. Verify those items after release rather than assuming every bonus follows the base game.
