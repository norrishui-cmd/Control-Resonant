## Quick answer

**Control Resonant is not a traditional open-world game.** Remedy describes its Manhattan as a collection of distinct, handcrafted zones, each with its own encounters, lore, secrets and optional activities. The stated goal is exploration without a huge checklist map.

## How the world is structured

The game leaves the Oldest House for a warped Manhattan. Architecture and gravity distort familiar streets into combat and traversal spaces. Within the zones, players can pursue the main campaign, self-contained World Quests, combat encounters, environmental challenges, hidden discoveries and Altered Items.

That design sounds more open-ended than the first Control while remaining curated. It should not be labeled a seamless city-wide sandbox unless Remedy later uses that description.

## What “expansive” means here

Remedy calls Resonant its most expansive game to date. Expansive describes scale and available exploration; it does not override the studio's explicit explanation that the game is built from carefully designed zones rather than a conventional open world.

## Will there be a map?

Preview material references areas and exploration, but the final map interface and zone list are not fully documented. Our [Manhattan zones guide](/guides/manhattan-map-and-zones/) records only named or officially described locations. For activity types, see [World Quests and campaign structure](/guides/world-quests-and-campaign/).

Remedy has also called the game “open-ended,” which describes choice and exploration across interconnected or handcrafted zones. It still does not mean a single seamless open-world map, and the two phrases should not be treated as interchangeable.
