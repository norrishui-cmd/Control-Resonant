## Quick answer

**Control Resonant lets Dylan use the environment as part of combat**, combining telekinetic powers, elemental abilities, the shapeshifting Aberrant and high mobility. Manhattan's distorted architecture and gravity anomalies turn spaces into tactical arenas rather than static backdrops.

## What “use the environment” confirms

Official descriptions explicitly include the environment among Dylan's combat tools. Hands-on coverage also reports a boss throwing road signs, girders and concrete, while Dylan's movement kit helps him react. This supports environmental interaction without proving that every visible object can be launched or destroyed.

## Melee and telekinesis

Resonant shifts the combat center toward melee, but it does not remove Control's paranatural identity. The Aberrant handles direct attack forms, while supernatural abilities and telekinetic options add range, control and interaction with space.

## Gravity as a tactical layer

Gravity anomalies distort both vertical and horizontal planes. Remedy says this adds dimensions to combat decisions as well as puzzles. Dashes, double jump and brief levitation let Dylan respond to those altered spaces.

## What remains to test

Final object damage, Launch costs, elemental reactions, destructibility and enemy resistances are not fully documented. A launch guide should identify repeatable mechanics rather than treating cinematic destruction as a universal system.
