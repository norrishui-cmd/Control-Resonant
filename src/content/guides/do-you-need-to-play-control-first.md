If you're eyeing Control Resonant but never finished — or never started — the original, you can relax. Remedy has been clear that the sequel is built to welcome newcomers.

## The short answer

**No, you do not need to play the first Control.** Resonant centers on a new protagonist, Dylan Faden, and tells a story designed to stand on its own. The studio has framed it as an entry point, not a continuation that punishes you for missing the setup.

## What newcomers should know going in

A few concepts carry over and are worth holding in your head:

- The **Federal Bureau of Control (FBC)** is a secret government agency that studies and contains paranatural phenomena.
- The **Hiss** is a hostile, reality-corrupting force — the red, whispering threat from the first game.
- **Objects of Power** and **Altered Items** are ordinary things warped into something dangerous and useful.

The game introduces what you actually need as you go, so you're not expected to arrive with a lore degree.

## What returning players get

Playing the original isn't required, but it does add texture:

- **Dylan and Jesse's relationship.** Jesse was the first game's hero; here she's missing, and that absence lands harder if you know her.
- **Callbacks to the Oldest House and the Hiss invasion**, which Resonant treats as recent history.
- **Connected-universe threads.** Remedy links its games together, and the Control series brushes up against the studio's other worlds — including Alan Wake's. Fans will catch nods that newcomers can simply enjoy as atmosphere.

## A practical recommendation

If you have the time and interest, the original Control (ideally with its two expansions) is a great game and deepens everything Resonant is doing. If you don't, start with Resonant guilt-free. The sequel was made with exactly that choice in mind.
