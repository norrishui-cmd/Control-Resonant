## Quick answer

The official US PlayStation Store currently labels Control Resonant with **nine accessibility features**. They are divided into four groups: volume controls and mono audio; basic subtitles and large subtitles; advanced controller remapping, basic adjustable stick sensitivity and basic adjustable stick inversion; advanced adjustable difficulty and game pausing.

| Area | Features listed |
| --- | --- |
| Audio | Volume controls; mono audio |
| Subtitles | Basic subtitles; large subtitles |
| Controls | Advanced controller remapping; adjustable stick sensitivity; adjustable stick inversion |
| Gameplay | Advanced adjustable difficulty; game pausing |

PlayStation defines advanced remapping here as the ability to fully customize game controls. Adjustable difficulty allows players to customize the challenge level or activate assists individually. The page says the game can be paused during gameplay or cinematics when playing offline.

## Important subtitle wording

The store’s accessibility descriptor says basic subtitles cover the main story and main characters. That wording should not be expanded into a guarantee that every ambient line, optional NPC or background audio cue is captioned. Likewise, “large subtitles” confirms a larger font option but not every possible visual customization such as background opacity or speaker color.

## Platform scope

This list is directly verified for the PS5 product page. Comparable settings may exist on PC and Xbox, but platform-specific accessibility labels should be checked on their own store or in the final settings menu. DualSense vibration and trigger effects and PS5 Pro enhancement are platform features listed separately, not part of the nine-count accessibility label.

Because the game has not launched, exact assist sliders and their effect on trophies remain unknown. Read the broader [accessibility guide](/guides/accessibility-options/) and [PS5 platform guide](/guides/control-resonant-ps5/) for related information.
