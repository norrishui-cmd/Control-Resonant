## Quick answer

Control Resonant has a **Standard Edition** and a **Digital Deluxe Edition**. On the US PlayStation Store they are listed at **$59.99** and **$69.99** respectively. Regional prices and physical-retailer prices vary.

## Edition comparison

| Content | Standard | Digital Deluxe |
| --- | :---: | :---: |
| Base game | Yes | Yes |
| AWE Mission Outfit | No | Yes |
| Untapped Artifact (Wallet) | No | Yes |
| Starter Resource Bundle | No | Yes |
| Digital artbook | No | Yes |
| Original soundtrack | No | Yes |

On PlayStation 5, Sony and Remedy also confirm **48 hours of advance access** for the Digital Deluxe Edition, with the store release time shown as September 22 rather than September 24. Do not assume that timing applies to every storefront unless its own listing says so.

The Deluxe extras currently presented by PlayStation are cosmetic, resource and digital-media items; no story expansion or season pass is listed in the edition contents.

## Pre-order bonus

The bonus differs by storefront. Steam and Xbox list the **Hiss Corruption Outfit** and **Pickpocket's Tool Artifact**. The US PlayStation listing also shows an **Occult Outfit**. Check the product page for your platform and region before buying rather than assuming all three items are universal.

## Are physical editions available?

Remedy says digital and physical pre-orders are available. Stock, formats and retailer-specific terms depend on platform and region. Check whether a box contains a disc or code before ordering.

## Which edition should you buy?

Choose Standard if you only want the game. Deluxe is the relevant upgrade if the outfit, artifact, resource bundle, artbook and soundtrack are worth the price difference to you. There is no confirmed gameplay expansion in the listed Deluxe package. See the [release-date page](/release-date/) for platforms and timing.
