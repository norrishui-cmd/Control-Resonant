## Quick answer

Control Resonant's combat loop is built around momentum: **melee hits restore the Combat Ability resource, abilities can stun enemies, and executions temporarily increase melee damage.** Continuing to attack feeds the tools needed to stay aggressive.

## The official combat loop

1. Use the Aberrant to land melee hits.
2. Restore the resource needed for supernatural Combat Abilities.
3. Spend abilities to damage or stun targets.
4. Execute stunned enemies when an opening appears.
5. Use the temporary melee-damage boost to maintain pressure.

This is not a promise that every ability stuns every target. Enemy resistance, exact thresholds and execution conditions require testing in the final build.

## Weapon and ability synergy

The Aberrant shifts among Primary Forms, Secondary Forms and Combo Enders. A heavy form can emphasize impact, while a faster form can build pressure and resources. Combat Abilities add elemental, control or summon effects. Talents and Artifacts then reinforce the chosen loop.

## Does aggression mean no defensive play?

No. Remedy says multiple playstyles are supported. Aggression describes the resource economy, not a requirement to ignore positioning. Dylan's dash, double jump and brief levitation make movement part of defense, while summons such as Mold Turrets can create a more tactical approach.

## Healing and enemy drops

A PlayStation hands-on preview reports that defeated enemies drop healing items, continuing the first game's incentive to move forward rather than wait behind cover. Exact health-drop rules and difficulty scaling are not yet documented.
