## Quick answer

**Seekers are a confirmed summon ability shown in Control Resonant's official loadout interface.** The description identifies them as volatile telekinetic entities that attack enemies independently and can be thrown and detonated.

## What Seekers do

The ability combines autonomous pressure with direct interaction. After summoning a Seeker, Dylan can allow it to attack or use telekinesis to reposition and detonate it. That creates a different decision from a passive summon that remains fixed in one place.

## Damage types shown

Official interface imagery displayed projectile and explosion damage, Falter values, a resource cost and a separate Launch resource cost. Those numbers came from a pre-release build and are deliberately not reproduced here as final stats. The useful confirmed distinction is that the summon has both projectile and explosive behavior.

## How it fits a build

Seekers use a Combat Ability slot rather than an Aberrant Weapon Form slot. Melee attacks restore the ability resource, so a summon-focused setup still needs a way to return to close-range pressure. Talents and Artifacts can potentially reinforce ability/melee synergy, but the final compatible nodes remain unknown.

## Seekers versus Mold Turrets

Both demonstrate tactical summon play, but their confirmed behavior differs. Mold Turrets apply harmful status effects from an independent position; Seekers can be thrown and detonated. A proper comparison requires final duration, cost and damage testing.
