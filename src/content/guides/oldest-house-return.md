## Quick answer

**Yes, Control Resonant returns to the Oldest House.** The sequel's larger setting is a warped Manhattan, but official PlayStation coverage says players will revisit previously unseen areas of the Bureau headquarters from the first Control.

## What happened to the Oldest House?

A hands-on preview describes the Oldest House as having fallen to the Hiss after a seven-year stalemate, forcing remaining FBC agents to evacuate into New York City. Steam's official description says Jesse Faden's lockdown is no longer holding and the catastrophe has spilled onto Manhattan's streets.

## Is it still the main map?

No. Remedy has deliberately inverted the first game's structure. Control focused on isolation inside a shifting brutalist building; Resonant moves across distinct city zones. The Oldest House remains important to the story and contains unseen areas, but it is not presented as the sole or primary world.

## Which sectors return?

No final sector list, checkpoint map or exploration percentage is public. “Previously unseen areas” confirms new Oldest House content without confirming that every original sector can be revisited.

## Why the return matters

The location connects Dylan's confinement, Jesse's leadership, the Hiss invasion and the FBC's failed containment to the new Manhattan emergency. It also gives returning players a familiar architectural language while keeping the sequel's main exploration fresh.
