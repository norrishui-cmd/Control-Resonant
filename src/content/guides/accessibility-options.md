## Quick answer

The US PlayStation Store currently lists nine accessibility features for Control Resonant: individual volume controls, mono audio, basic subtitles, large subtitles, advanced controller remapping, basic stick-sensitivity adjustment, basic stick inversion, advanced difficulty adjustment and game pausing.

## Audio

- **Volume controls:** individual audio categories can be lowered or muted.
- **Mono audio:** the same audio can be sent to both speakers.

## Subtitles and captions

- **Basic subtitles:** the store says subtitles are included for the main story and main characters.
- **Large subtitles:** a larger subtitle size is available.

The listing does not currently confirm closed captions for every ambient sound, direction indicator or speaker name.

## Controls

- **Advanced controller remapping:** controls can be fully customized.
- **Stick sensitivity:** some sensitivity options are provided.
- **Stick inversion:** some inversion options are provided.

Remapping is particularly relevant because Resonant combines melee forms, abilities and multi-step aerial movement. The final input list should be checked on the release build.

## Gameplay

- **Advanced adjustable difficulty:** multiple gameplay settings can be adjusted.
- **Game pausing:** play can be paused.

The store classification does not publish every individual difficulty slider or assist. Avoid assuming invulnerability, timing windows or puzzle assists until Remedy documents them.

## Platform scope

These features are confirmed by the PS5 listing. PC, Xbox and Mac may support overlapping options, but their final accessibility matrices should be verified separately. For language and subtitle availability, see the [language support guide](/guides/language-support/).
