## Quick answer

**Artifacts are equippable items that apply passive modifiers to Dylan's build.** They can affect survivability, combat performance, exploration, or the resource economy. Some include conditions or trade-offs, so the system is intended for situational buildcraft rather than simple, automatic power increases.

Players can equip up to **three artifacts during the first playthrough**. A fourth slot opens in New Game Plus.

## How artifact crafting works

Players discover **Untapped Artifacts** throughout the world. These are crafted into usable artifacts inside **The Gap**, the metaphysical build space connected to Dylan's psyche. Remedy has not yet published material costs, rarity tiers, reroll rules, or a complete artifact list.

The safe pre-release interpretation is therefore:

1. Find an Untapped Artifact while exploring.
2. Enter The Gap.
3. Craft it into an equippable artifact.
4. Compare its passive modifier, condition, and trade-off against the current build.

Any more specific recipe or farming claim requires the release build.

## What artifact effects can influence

Official examples are described as categories, not named final items: survival, combat, exploration and resource economy. This creates several build roles. A fragile damage setup may use survival modifiers; an ability-heavy setup may care more about resources; exploration-focused choices could be useful while clearing optional content.

## Artifact slots and New Game Plus

The first run's three-slot limit forces trade-offs. New Game Plus adds a fourth slot, enabling more complex interactions alongside new talent nodes and expanded boss-ability combinations. Existing artifacts carry into the new run.

## Deluxe and pre-order artifacts

The Digital Deluxe Edition lists an **Untapped Artifact (Wallet)**, while the pre-order offer lists the **Pickpocket's Tool Artifact**. Their exact gameplay effects should not be guessed before official descriptions or verified in-game data appear.
